## 2026

**Gini C**, Byrd MKH, Ahsin M, Ali L, Virginio Da Silva J, Correia de Almeida Regitano L, Van Vliet S, Brito LF, Johnson JS, Jiang J, Tiezzi F, Maltecca C.
*Physiological axes drive gut microbiome genomic, transcriptional, and metabolic functions in heat-stressed lactating sows.*
Under revision — ISME Communications.

> Lactating sows under heat stress are under enormous physiological pressure — they need to produce milk, regulate body temperature, and maintain immune function all at once. In this study, we asked: how does this juggling act reshape the gut microbiome, not just in terms of who's there, but what they're actually *doing*? By integrating metagenomic, metatranscriptomic, and metabolomic data, we identified clear microbial functional signatures linked to key physiological axes in the sow, offering a new lens through which to understand heat stress resilience in livestock.

**Gini C**, Tiezzi F, Jiang J, Byrd MKH, Wen H, Johnson JS, Brito LF, van Vliet S, Maltecca C.
*Data-driven enterosignatures link gut microbiome reorganization to heat stress responses in lactating sows.*
*Frontiers in Microbiology* 17:1797687. [doi: 10.3389/fmicb.2026.1797687](https://doi.org/10.3389/fmicb.2026.1797687)

> Think of the gut microbiome as an ecosystem that reorganizes itself under pressure. When lactating sows face heat stress, we found that their gut microbial communities don't just shift randomly — they reorganize into distinct, reproducible configurations we call "enterosignatures." These signatures are tightly linked to how the animal responds to thermal challenge, opening the door to using microbiome profiles as early indicators of heat stress resilience in pig production.

Sabatino R†, **Gini C**†, Borgomaneiro G, Sbaffi T, Corno G, Sun K, Zhang XH, Di Cesare A.
*Sinking Particles as Repository of Antimicrobial Resistome Diversity in the Sansha Yongle Blue Hole.*
*Science of the Total Environment* 1021:181585. [doi: 10.1016/j.scitotenv.2026.181585](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.scitotenv.2026.181585)

> Blue holes are some of the most extreme and mysterious marine environments on Earth — isolated, oxygen-depleted, and largely unexplored. We looked at the sinking particles drifting through the Sansha Yongle Blue Hole in the South China Sea and found them to be surprisingly rich reservoirs of antimicrobial resistance genes. This tells us that even in the most remote ocean environments, the fingerprint of antimicrobial resistance is present — carried and concentrated by the slow rain of organic matter sinking to the depths.

## 2025

Maltecca C, **Gini C**, Wen H, Byrd MKH, Van Vliet S, Brito LF, Johnson JS, Jiang J, Tiezzi F.
*Functional Guild Analysis Characterizes Gut Microbiome Responses to Heat Stress in Thermotolerant and Thermosensitive Lactating Sows Across Three Omics Layers.*
Under revision — Journal of Animal Science and Biotechnology.

> Not all sows respond to heat the same way — some cope remarkably well, while others struggle. In this study, we grouped gut microbes into functional guilds based on what they do rather than what they are, and tracked how these guilds behave differently in heat-tolerant versus heat-sensitive animals. By looking across three omics layers simultaneously, we got a much richer picture of the microbial strategies at play — and the biological levers that might one day help us breed more resilient pigs.

Nehme R†, **Gini C**†, Vanbergue E, Even S, Biscarini F, Andrés S, Rault L, Noel F, Hardit V, Bouhallab S, Pereira DM, Ksouri R, Roussel P, López S, Cremonesi P, Castiglioni B, Caruso D, Faré F, Fontana M, Ceciliani F, Abdennebi-Najar L.
*The effects of Thymus capitatus essential oil topical application on milk quality: a systems biology approach.*
*Scientific Reports* 15(1):4627. [doi: 10.1038/s41598-025-88168-0](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41598-025-88168-0)

> Could a plant-derived essential oil improve the quality of dairy milk? We applied *Thymus capitatus* essential oil topically to dairy animals and took a systems biology approach to understand the effects — looking at the microbiome, metabolome, and milk composition together. The results revealed that even a topical application can have measurable ripple effects on milk quality, highlighting the potential of natural compounds as alternatives to conventional treatments in dairy production.

Morasi RM, Biscarini F, **Gini C**, Ceciliani F, Lecchi C, Salina A, Thaler Neto A, Langoni H, Gebara C, Crippa BL, Silva NC.
*Assessment of bacterial diversity in bovine bulk tank milk samples from Brazil.*
*Brazilian Journal of Microbiology* 56(4):2507–20.

> Bulk tank milk is a snapshot of the bacterial world of an entire herd. In this collaborative study with Brazilian colleagues, we used next-generation sequencing to characterize the bacterial diversity in milk tanks across multiple farms, painting a detailed picture of the microbial landscape of Brazilian dairy production and its implications for milk safety and quality.

Bao Q, Wen H, Zeng L, et al. (**Gini C**, co-author)
*Long-read sequencing reveals the impact of structural variation on gene expression and complex traits in pigs.*
Under revision — *Nature Genetics*.

> The pig genome hides a layer of complexity that short-read sequencing simply cannot capture: large structural variants — inversions, duplications, deletions — that can silently rewire gene expression and shape economically important traits. Using long-read sequencing technology, this large international consortium study maps these structural variants at unprecedented resolution, revealing their widespread functional impact and opening new avenues for genomic selection in pig breeding.

Bao Q, Zeng L, Wen H, et al. (**Gini C**, co-author)
*PIGSVOME: an integrated resource of porcine genomic structural variants for functional genomics and breeding applications.*
Under revision — *Journal of Genetics and Genomics*.

> As a companion resource to our structural variation study, PIGSVOME is a comprehensive, openly accessible database of porcine structural variants. It is designed to serve as a reference tool for researchers working in functional genomics and animal breeding, making it easier to query, annotate, and leverage structural variation data in the pig genome.

## 2024

de Oliveira Duarte FA, Ramos KK, **Gini C**, Morasi RM, Silva NC, Efraim P.
*Microbiological characterization of kombucha and biocellulose film produced with black tea and cocoa bean shell infusion.*
*Food Research International* 190:114568. [doi: 10.1016/j.foodres.2024.114568](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.foodres.2024.114568)

> Kombucha is having a moment — but what exactly is living in your bottle? In this study, we characterized the microbial communities in kombucha and its biocellulose byproduct when brewed with an unusual combination of black tea and cocoa bean shell infusion. Beyond satisfying curiosity, this kind of work helps us understand fermentation dynamics and quality control in functional food production.

Andrés S, **Gini C**, Ceciliani F, Gutiérrez-Expósito D, Arteche-Villasol N, Martín A, Cremonesi P, Faré F, Ghaffari MH, Giráldez FJ, Abdennebi-Najar L.
*Essential oil supplementation in milk replacers: short- and long-term impacts on feed efficiency, the faecal microbiota and the plasma metabolome in dairy calves.*
*Journal of Developmental Origins of Health and Disease* 15:e5. [doi: 10.1017/S2040174424000084](https://doi.org/10.1017/S2040174424000084)

> Early life nutrition shapes an animal's microbiome — and potentially its health — for the long term. We supplemented milk replacers fed to young dairy calves with essential oils and followed the animals over time, tracking changes in gut microbiota, plasma metabolites, and feed efficiency. The results give us a clearer picture of how natural feed additives can influence the developmental trajectory of the calf microbiome.

*Comparison of bacterial diversity in wet- and dry-aged beef using traditional microbiology and next generation sequencing.*
*The Microbe* 2:100035. [doi: 10.1016/j.microb.2024.100035](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.microb.2024.100035)

## 2023

Ranilla MJ, Andrés S, **Gini C**, Biscarini F, Saro C, Martín A, Mateos I, López S, Giráldez FJ, Abdennebi-Najar L, Pereira D.
*Effects of Thymbra capitata essential oil on in vitro fermentation end-products and ruminal bacterial communities.*
*Scientific Reports* 13(1):4153. [doi: 10.1038/s41598-023-31144-3](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41598-023-31144-3)

> The rumen is one of nature's most impressive fermentation chambers, and what lives inside it profoundly affects livestock health and greenhouse gas emissions. We tested whether *Thymbra capitata* essential oil — a Mediterranean plant — could modulate ruminal fermentation and its microbial community in vitro. The findings suggest that plant-derived compounds could be promising tools for fine-tuning rumen function naturally.

## 2020

Lecchi C, Zamarian V, **Gini C**, Avanzini C, Polloni A, Rota Nodari S, Ceciliani F.
*Salivary microRNAs are potential biomarkers for the accurate and precise identification of inflammatory response after tail docking and castration in piglets.*
*Journal of Animal Science* 98(5):skaa153. [doi: 10.1093/jas/skaa153](https://doi.org/10.1093/jas/skaa153)

> Tail docking and castration are routine procedures in pig farming, but measuring the pain and stress they cause in young animals is notoriously difficult. In this study, we explored whether small RNA molecules found in saliva — microRNAs — could serve as non-invasive biomarkers of inflammatory response in piglets following these procedures. The answer was yes, opening a new, animal-friendly avenue for welfare monitoring in pig production.
