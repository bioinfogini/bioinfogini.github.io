Hi, I'm **Chiara** — a postdoctoral researcher in microbial genomics with a passion for understanding how microbial communities shape animal health and production.

I am currently based at the **Department of Animal Science, North Carolina State University (Raleigh, NC)**, where I work in the lab of Prof. Christian Maltecca. My research focuses on the gut microbiome of livestock — particularly lactating sows — using a multi-omics approach that integrates shotgun metagenomics, metatranscriptomics, and metabolomics to uncover how microbial communities respond to physiological and environmental stressors such as heat stress.

## Research Interests

My work sits at the intersection of **microbial ecology**, **bioinformatics**, and **animal science**. I am particularly interested in:

- Multi-omics integration (metagenomics, metatranscriptomics, metabolomics)
- Gut microbiome dynamics in livestock under heat stress
- Antimicrobial resistance in environmental microbial communities
- Bioinformatics pipeline development and benchmarking

## Background

I completed my **Ph.D. in Veterinary and Animal Science** at the University of Milan (Italy) in 2024, under the supervision of Prof. Fabrizio Ceciliani, where I developed expertise in amplicon sequencing and microbiome bioinformatics. Before joining NCSU, I was a visiting researcher at the **University of Guelph (Canada)** and a postdoctoral fellow at the **National Research Council of Italy (CNR-IRSA)**, where I studied antimicrobial resistance in marine environments.

My research has been presented at international conferences including **PAG**, **AGBT Ag**, and **ISME19**, and I have been the recipient of competitive travel awards from Scherago International and the Animal Breeding-RCN.
